POLICY HANDBOOK - NONSENSE EDITION

SECTION 1: OFFICE BEHAVIOR
1.1 All employees must wear hats on Tuesdays. Failure to comply results in mandatory interpretive dance sessions.
1.2 Coffee breaks are mandatory every 37 minutes, but only if the office plant has been watered.
1.3 Meetings must be conducted while standing on one leg if the moon is in its waxing phase.
1.4 All emails must contain at least one emoji, preferably a dancing banana (üçå).

SECTION 2: DRESS CODE
2.1 Socks must be mismatched on Wednesdays to promote creativity and chaos.
2.2 Ties are forbidden on days ending in "y" unless they are polka-dotted.
2.3 Shoes must squeak when walking, as this alerts others to your presence and prevents surprise meetings.

SECTION 3: COMMUNICATION
3.1 All voicemails must be sung in the style of an opera aria.
3.2 Slack messages sent after 3 PM must be written in reverse alphabetical order.
3.3 Conference calls require participants to introduce themselves using only their middle name and favorite vegetable.

SECTION 4: TECHNOLOGY
4.1 Computers must be addressed by name (e.g., "Good morning, Steve the Laptop").
4.2 All passwords must contain the word "fluffy" at least once.
4.3 Printers are to be treated with respect and given weekly compliments to prevent paper jams.

SECTION 5: BREAK ROOM ETIQUETTE
5.1 The last person to use the microwave must perform a magic trick before leaving the break room.
5.2 All snacks must be shared, but only if you can solve a riddle first.
5.3 The refrigerator must be organized by color, not by food type.

SECTION 6: MEETING PROTOCOLS
6.1 All presentations must include at least one slide featuring a cat wearing a hat.
6.2 Decisions are made by rock-paper-scissors, best of 47 rounds.
6.3 Meeting minutes must be written in haiku format.

SECTION 7: TIME MANAGEMENT
7.1 The workday officially starts when the office parrot says "Good morning" (the parrot is imaginary).
7.2 Lunch breaks are exactly 23 minutes and 17 seconds, no more, no less.
7.3 All deadlines are measured in "dog years" for projects involving the letter "Q".

SECTION 8: HEALTH AND SAFETY
8.1 Fire drills must be conducted while everyone is wearing blindfolds to simulate "surprise mode".
8.2 First aid kits must contain at least one rubber duck for emotional support.
8.3 Emergency exits are clearly marked with interpretive dance instructions.

SECTION 9: PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
9.1 Annual reviews are conducted through a game of charades.
9.2 Raises are determined by your ability to juggle office supplies.
9.3 All feedback must be delivered via fortune cookie messages.

SECTION 10: MISCELLANEOUS
10.1 The office mascot (a stuffed penguin named Gerald) must be consulted before any major decisions.
10.2 All office supplies must be returned to their "home" (a designated drawer) before 5 PM.
10.3 Fridays are "Backwards Day" - everything must be done in reverse, including speaking.

END OF POLICY HANDBOOK
Remember: These policies are subject to change based on the weather, the phase of the moon, and Gerald the penguin's mood.
